Catalog QuickSearch is a new way to search the University Libraries' articles, books, and other resources. Catalog QuickSearch is automatically limited to items held locally, but can be expanded to include articles by switching tabs to "CatalogPlus." Useful Web 2.0 Features A Google-like single search box An Amazon-like display An index of millions of articles The Tag Cloud Community tagging (add your own descriptors) Image of selected books What's Covered by a Catalog QuickSearch search? The Library Catalog Most subscription article databases Several Open Access resources Does Catalog QuickSearch replace Catalog Classic? No, Catalog QuickSearch enhances the traditional Library Catalog. While it's a super keyword searcher, you may still want to use the Catalog Classic for searches where you want to exercise a great degree of control or for specific author, title, or combinations of searches.

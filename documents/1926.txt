Clickers are the common term for Student Response System (SRS), a technology used to promote active learning in classrooms. TurningTechnologies, is the University wide standard clicker solution for the UofM. In educational settings, clickers can be used to encourage student participation in what has been, traditionally passive, lecture-style classrooms. It allows the class to vote on a topic, take a survey, ask or answer a question and respond to attendance. The responses are tabulated and instructors can view, save, and (if they wish) display anonymously for the entire class to see. Where Do I Begin? First, be sure that your class/instructor requires a clicker. You can do this be simply asking, checking the course syllabus or checking the book list for the class To Purchase a clicker: Clickers should be purchased at the Official Campus Bookstore in the V. Lane Rawlins Service Court, across from the University Center. Each product will come with instructions. Consult these to learn how to set up your clicker.

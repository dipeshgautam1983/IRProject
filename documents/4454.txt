Humphreys School of Law About Admissions Programs Current Students Faculty Careers Library Faculty &amp; Staff Full-Time Faculty Adjunct Faculty Emeritus Faculty Faculty News Home School of Law Faculty John Newman John Newman Assistant Professor of Law Phone (901) 678-3224 Email jmnwman1@memphis.edu Fax Office Law School, Office 351 Office Hours CV SSRN About Professor Newman Professor Newman joined the University of Memphis law faculty in 2014, where he currently teaches first-year Contracts, Conflict of Laws, and Antitrust Law. His work focuses on competition and innovation in technology markets. As a trial attorney with the U.S. Department of Justice in Washington, D.C., Professor Newman's primary practice focused on multidistrict civil antitrust litigation, reviewing the competitive effects of mergers and acquisitions, and investigating potential antitrust violations. He also maintained an active pro bono practice, representing low-income tenants in litigation and alternative dispute resolution and advising a start-up on intellectual-property licensing issues. While at the University of Iowa College of Law, Professor Newman served as research assistant to Herbert Hovenkamp, was managing editor of the Iowa Law Review , and published student notes in journals at the University of Iowa and the University of Virginia. Education J. D., University of Iowa College of Law (2011); B.A., Iowa State University of Science &amp; Technology (2007). Admitted Iowa Bar and the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia. Experience Practice: Trial Attorney, U.S. Department of Justice, Antitrust Division, 2011-2014; D.C. Bar Advocacy &amp; Justice Clinic, 2012-2014. Academic: Visiting Assistant Professor, University of Memphis Cecil C. Humphreys School of Law, 2014-2015; Assistant Professor, University of Memphis Cecil C. Humphreys School of Law, 2015-present. Teaching Interests Contracts, Conflict of Laws, Antitrust Law. Selected Publications Antitrust in Zero-Price Markets: Foundations, 164 U. Pennsylvania L. Rev. (forthcoming). Copyright Freeconomics, 66 Vanderbilt L. Rev. 1409 (2013). Personal Jurisdiction and Choice of Law in the Cloud, 73 Maryland L. Rev. 313 (2013) (with Damon C. Andrews). Anticompetitive Product Design in the New Economy, 39 Florida St. U. L. Rev. 681 (2012).
